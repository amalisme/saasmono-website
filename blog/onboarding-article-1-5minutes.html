Step 1: Verify Your Email (2 minutes)

This enables all alert notifications

Why This Matters

If you don't verify your email, you won't receive ANY alerts. Your site could be down for hours and you'd never know. This is the most common mistake new users make.

How to Do It

1. Check your email inbox

Look for an email from UptimeRobot with subject: "Please verify your email address"

2. Click the verification link

Opens a new browser window confirming verification

3. See confirmation message

Should say "Email verified successfully"

‚ùì Didn't get the email?

  • ‚Ä¢ Check spam/junk folder
  • ‚Ä¢ Wait 5 minutes (sometimes delayed)
  • ‚Ä¢ Log into UptimeRobot ‚Üí My Settings ‚Üí Alert Contacts ‚Üí Resend verification
  • ‚Ä¢ Make sure you used the correct email when signing up

Step 2: Add Your First Monitor (3 minutes)

Start tracking your homepage

What You're Monitoring

Your first monitor should be your homepage. It's the most important page - if this is down, your whole site is likely down. We'll add more pages later.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1 Click "Add New Monitor"

Big green button on your dashboard. Can't miss it.

2 Choose "HTTP(s)" as Monitor Type

This works for all websites. Don't overthink it - just select HTTP(s).

Note: Other types (Ping, Port, Keyword) are for advanced users. Start with HTTP(s).

3 Enter a Friendly Name

Use something clear like "Main Site - Homepage" or "MyStore.com Homepage"

‚úì Good: "Main Site - Homepage"

‚úì Good: "E-commerce Store Homepage"

‚úó Bad: "Monitor 1"

‚úó Bad: "Website"

4 Enter Your URL

Copy and paste your full homepage URL. Include https://

https://yourwebsite.com
Tip: Don't just type "mysite" - use the complete URL with https://

5 Set Monitoring Interval

Leave it at 5 minutes (default for free plan)

This means your site gets checked every 5 minutes. If it goes down, you'll know within 5 minutes max. That's plenty for most businesses.

6 Leave Other Settings Default

Don't change anything else. The defaults work great for beginners.

7 Click "Create Monitor"

Bottom of the form. Green button. Done!

üéâ Success!

You should now see your monitor on the dashboard. It will say "Up" with a green checkmark if your site is working. Within 5 minutes, UptimeRobot will check your site for the first time.

Step 3: Confirm Alert Settings (1 minute)

Make sure you'll actually get notified

Why This Step Matters

Your email is already added as an alert contact, but we need to make sure it's configured correctly. Many people skip this and wonder why they never get alerts.

Quick Check

1. Go to "My Settings"

Click your profile icon (top right) ‚Üí My Settings

2. Click "Alert Contacts"

Left sidebar menu

3. Check Your Email Status

You should see your email with a green checkmark and "Active" status

‚úì If verified: You're all set!

‚úó If not verified: Go back to Step 1 and verify your email

Common First-Timer Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Mistake #1: Not Verifying Email

Why it's bad: You won't get ANY alerts. Your site could be down for days.
Fix: Check your email right now and click that verification link.

‚ùå Mistake #2: Using Generic Monitor Names

Why it's bad: When you get an alert, "Monitor 1 is down" tells you nothing.
Fix: Use descriptive names like "Main Site - Homepage" or "Store Checkout Page"

‚ùå Mistake #3: Wrong URL Format

Why it's bad: Monitor won't work if URL is wrong.
Fix: Use full URL with https:// - Copy it directly from your browser.

‚ùå Mistake #4: Only Monitoring Homepage

Why it's bad: Homepage might be up while checkout/login is broken.
Fix: Add more monitors (we'll cover this in Article #2)
