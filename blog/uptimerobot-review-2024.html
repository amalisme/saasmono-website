What is UptimeRobot?

UptimeRobot is a website monitoring service that checks if your site is up and running. When something goes wrong, it alerts you immediately via email, SMS, voice call, email to SMS, and mobile app push. You also can integrate with third party integrations such as Slack, Zapier, Telegram, webhooks, PagerDuty, Discord, Splunk On-Call, Mattermost, Pushbullet, Microsoft Teams, Google chat, Pushover, and if you need more, you can suggest and vote. In addition to that, you also can connect your UptimeRobot monitors to PagerTree, Pipedream, ilert alerts, and Zenduty.

Think of it as a watchdog for your website. Instead of constantly checking your site yourself (or worse, finding out from customers), UptimeRobot does it automatically every few minutes.

It can monitor HTTP(S) websites, ping servers, check specific ports, watch for keywords on pages, and even track SSL certificate expiration dates. Basically, if it can break on your website, UptimeRobot can probably monitor it.

The service has been around since 2010 and is trusted by over 1 million websites worldwide. It's one of the most popular monitoring solutions for small to medium-sized businesses.

What I Wish I Knew Earlier

A few things I learned along the way:

  • • Monitor your checkout separately - Your homepage might be up while checkout is broken. Monitor both.

  • • Create a status page - Even if you think you don't need it, customers appreciate transparency.
  • • Don't over-monitor - I started with 15 monitors. Turns out I only needed 5. Keep it simple.

Free vs Paid: When to Upgrade?

I've been on the free plan for 6 months and haven't felt the need to upgrade. But here's when you should consider Pro ($7/month):

✅ Stick with Free If:

  • • You're a side project, blog, or portfolio site
  • • 5-minute checks are acceptable (most businesses)
  • • Email/SMS alerts are enough
  • • You don't need advanced reporting
  • • You're testing out monitoring for the first time
  • • Your revenue doesn't critically depend on uptime

⬆️ Upgrade to Pro If:

  • • You're e-commerce or SaaS (revenue = uptime)
  • • You need 1-minute checks for faster detection
  • • You want Slack, Discord, or webhook alerts
  • • You need maintenance windows (planned downtime)
  • • You want advanced logs and reports
  • • You manage multiple client sites
  • • 5 minutes of downtime = significant revenue loss

My take: Start with free. Use it for a month. If you find yourself wishing for faster checks or more alert options, upgrade then. No rush.

For context: I run a small e-commerce store that does about $10K/month. The free plan is enough for me because 5 minutes of downtime costs me maybe $30-50 max. If I was doing $100K/month, I'd upgrade to 1-minute checks immediately.
