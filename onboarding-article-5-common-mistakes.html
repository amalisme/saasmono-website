#2

Monitoring without notifications is useless

‚ùå Why It's Wrong

If your email isn't verified, alerts go nowhere. UptimeRobot is monitoring your site and detecting downtime, but you never find out. Your site could be down for hours or days without you knowing.

Scenario 2: Typo in Email Address

You skip verification thinking you'll come back to it. You forget. Weeks go by with zero alerts.

    • ‚Ä¢ Check spam/junk folder
    • ‚Ä¢ Search for "verify your email"
  3. 3. Resend if needed:
    • ‚Ä¢ Alert Contacts ‚Üí Resend Verification

    • ‚Ä¢ Check spam again
  4. 4. Test alerts:

‚ùå Why It's Wrong

  • ‚Ä¢ Sudden panic: You find out when customers complain

    • ‚Ä¢ Better: 14 days (more time to fix)
    • ‚Ä¢ Best: 30 days (plenty of buffer)
  3. 3. Check Expiration Date:
    • ‚Ä¢ View monitor details
    • ‚Ä¢ See when SSL expires
    • ‚Ä¢ Add reminder to calendar

#7

Wrong Alert Threshold Settings

Either alert fatigue or missed emergencies

‚ùå Why It's Wrong

Two opposite problems from the same mistake:

Problem 1: Threshold Too Low (1 check)

Problem 2: Threshold Too High (5+ checks)

‚úÖ How to Fix It

Use the right threshold for each situation:

‚úì Best for Most: 2 Checks

  • ‚Ä¢ Filters out random blips
  • ‚Ä¢ Still alerts quickly (5-10 min with 5-min intervals)
  • ‚Ä¢ Good balance of accuracy and speed
  • ‚Ä¢ Use for: Most monitors

‚ö° For Critical Pages: 1 Check (Use Sparingly)

  • ‚Ä¢ Instant alerts (no delay)
  • ‚Ä¢ More false positives
  • ‚Ä¢ Use for: Checkout, payment processing only
  • ‚Ä¢ Warning: Expect occasional false alarms

üê¢ For Non-Critical: 3 Checks

  • ‚Ä¢ Very few false positives
  • ‚Ä¢ Takes longer to alert (10-15 min)
  • ‚Ä¢ Use for: Blog posts, about pages, secondary features

  1. 1. Start with 2 checks for everything
  2. 2. Track false positives for a week:
    • ‚Ä¢ Getting lots of false alarms? Increase to 3 checks
    • ‚Ä¢ No false alarms? You're good
  3. 3. Adjust critical monitors:
    • ‚Ä¢ Checkout/payment: Consider 1 check
    • ‚Ä¢ Accept you'll get occasional false alarms
